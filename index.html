📋 Info & Statistics
🌍 What is the Strait of Hormuz?
A narrow sea passage between Iran and Oman/UAE. It is the world's most important oil shipping route — every oil tanker going to or from the Persian Gulf must pass through here.

📌 Quick Facts
📍 Location26°N, 56°E
📏 Narrowest point~39 km wide
🌊 Water depth60 – 100 metres
🏳 BordersIran · Oman · UAE
🚢 Ships per day50 – 100 ships
🛢 Oil per day~21 million barrels
⚡ % of world oil~20% of supply
🛣 How Ships Travel Through
Ships must follow strict lanes — like a motorway at sea:
🟢 Entering the Gulf
Ships heading INTO the Persian Gulf travel along the Oman coastline (right side)
🔴 Leaving the Gulf
Ships heading OUT towards the open sea travel along the Iran coastline (left side)
⚪ Safety Gap
A 3.2 km empty zone between the two lanes — like a motorway central reservation
